About Pulau Pangkor

Welcome to Pulau Pangkor in Perak

Pulau Pangkor is a small island located off the west coast of Perak in Malaysia.

Known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant fishing villages, and rich cultural heritage, it is a popular tourist destination

Get to know about Pulau Pangkor

Pulau Pangkor spans approximately 18 square kilometers and is surrounded by the Straits of Malacca. The island has a tropical rainforest climate, with warm temperatures and high humidity throughout the year.


Attractions and Activities

Beaches

Pangkor is famous for its pristine beaches, including Teluk Nipah, Coral Beach, and Pasir Bogak. These beaches offer clear waters, white sand, and opportunities for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.

Pangkor Laut Resort

A luxury resort located on a private island just off the coast of Pangkor, known for its exclusive amenities and natural beauty.

Wildlife and Nature

The island's interior is covered in lush rainforest, home to diverse flora and fauna. Visitors can explore jungle trails, observe hornbills, and enjoy the island's natural scenery.

Fishing Villages

Pangkor has several traditional fishing villages where visitors can experience the local lifestyle and try fresh seafood.

Water Sports

Popular activities include kayaking, jet-skiing, and banana boat rides. Snorkeling and diving spots around the island offer views of vibrant coral reefs and marine life.

Accessibility

Pulau Pangkor is accessible by ferry from Lumut, a town on the mainland of Perak. The ferry ride takes about 30 to 40 minutes. There are also domestic flights to Pangkor Airport, primarily from Kuala Lumpur.
