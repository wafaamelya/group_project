Welcome to Batu Ferringhi, Pulau Pinang

Batu Ferringhi is a popular beach destination located on the northern coast of Penang Island (Pulau Pinang), Malaysia.

Known for its beautiful sandy beaches, vibrant nightlife, and a variety of water sports, Batu Ferringhi is a favorite spot for both tourists and locals.

Get to know about Batu Ferringhi

Batu Ferringhi, while primarily known today as a popular tourist destination, has an interesting historical background that reflects the broader historical context of Penang Island and Malaysia.


Early History

The name "Batu Ferringhi" is derived from Malay words, where "Batu" means rock and "Ferringhi" refers to foreigners or Westerners. Historically, "Ferringhi" was used by Malays to describe Portuguese and Dutch traders who arrived in the region during the 16th and 17th centuries.

Colonial Era

Penang Island, including Batu Ferringhi, became part of the British Empire in the late 18th century. Captain Francis Light established George Town as a British trading post in 1786.

Post-Independence Development

After Malaysia gained independence in 1957, Penang saw significant development, and Batu Ferringhi gradually transformed into a major tourist destination. The construction of hotels, resorts and other tourism infrastructure began in earnest from the 1970s onwards.

Modern Era

Today, Batu Ferringhi is a well-established tourist hub attracting visitors from around the world. It offers a blend of natural beauty, cultural richness and modern amenities, making it a unique destination within Malaysia.


Batu Ferringhi's history is intertwined with the broader historical narrative of Penang and Malaysia, reflecting periods of colonial influence, economic development, and cultural integration. From its early days as a fishing village to its current status as a premier tourist destination, Batu Ferringhi has evolved while retaining elements of its rich historical and cultural heritage.

Key highlight about Batu Ferringhi


    Beaches and Water Activities:

  • Batu Ferringhi's beaches are lined with golden sand and are perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and relaxing. Also famous for its water sports activities, including parasailing, jet skiing, banana boat rides and windsurfing.

    • Night Market:

  • As the sun sets, the bustling night market comes alive. Stretching along the main road, the market features numerous stalls selling a variety of goods, including clothing, souvenirs, accessories and local crafts.

    • Dining and Cuisine:

  • Visitors can enjoy local Malaysian cuisine, fresh seafood, as well as international dishes. Along the beach and in the night market, you'll find numerous food stalls serving popular local dishes like char kway teow, satay, laksa, and more.
