Description of Mount Jerai

Mount Jerai, also known as Gunung Jerai is a significant peak in Malaysia's state of Kedah. Mount Jerai is located in the Yan district, near the town of Gurun in Kedah. It stands 1,217 metres above sea level. Mount Jerai is one of the tallest peaks on Malaysia's northern peninsula.


Furthermore, Mount Jerai has historically served as an important navigational point for sailors and traders travelling the Malacca Strait. It serves as a guide for ships going along the maritime trade routes that connect the Indian Ocean to the South China Sea. During ancient times, Mount Jerai was part of the Kedah Tua Kingdom. This kingdom, which flourished from the fourth to the twelfth centuries CE, was a key trading hub and port of call for traders from India, China, and the Arab World.
