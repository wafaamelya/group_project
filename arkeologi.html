About Bujang Valley Archaeological Museum

Welcome to Bujang Valley Archaeological Museum in Merbok, Kedah

Bujang Valley Archaeological Museum is near Sungai Batu, Bukit Batu Pahat, Merbok, Kedah. The Ministry of Information, Communication and Culture Malaysia administers the museum. This archaeological complex covers an area of ​​three square kilometers. It is located in an area of ​​palm oil plantations. 97 research sites are the key to unlocking the history of the country's early civilization. This museum displays the historical perspective of archaeology, anthropology, and sociology of Southeast Asia's high and old Malay community. This museum is located in the middle of the center of settlement, trade, and the development of the earliest civilization in the Malay Peninsula with monuments and inscribed stones over 1,900 years old found by a group of archaeologists from Universiti Sains Malaysia (USM). It later extended to the "Empire of SriVijaya". This museum complex holds various historical treasures and heirlooms that prove that Kedah is one of the oldest states in the region, which is believed to have existed since 110 AD. The Bujang Valley Archaeological Museum was built in 1978.
